FAQ

\r\n

Q1: What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?
A: AI is the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think, learn, and solve problems autonomously.

\r\n

Q2: How is AI used in daily life?
A:AI in Everyday Life\r\n

AI powers recommendations on streaming platforms, voice assistants like Siri and Alexa, and smart home devices. In healthcare, AI helps diagnose diseases, predict patient outcomes, and even assist in robotic surgeries. In business, it enhances decision-making, automates repetitive tasks, and provides insights from massive datasets.

\r\n

The Technology Behind AI

\r\n

AI relies on machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing. These technologies allow systems to “learn” from data, recognize patterns, and make predictions or decisions with minimal human intervention.

\r\n

Challenges and Ethical Concerns

\r\n

Despite its potential, AI brings challenges: data privacy concerns, job displacement due to automation, biases in AI models, and the risk of autonomous systems making critical decisions without human oversight. Researchers and policymakers are increasingly focused on creating ethical frameworks to guide AI development.

\r\n

Looking Ahead

\r\n

The rise of AI signals a new era of innovation. As AI becomes more capable, its influence on education, healthcare, transportation, and entertainment will deepen, transforming how we live, work, and interact with technology.

Q1: What is the Gen Z Revolution in Nepal?
A: The Gen Z Revolution refers to the social, cultural, and political movements led by Nepali youth born roughly between 1997 and 2012. It is characterized by digital activism, demands for social justice, and a push for government accountability.

\r\n

Q2: What are the main issues Gen Z is fighting for in Nepal?
A: Key issues include climate change, gender equality, LGBTQ+ rights, mental health awareness, anti-corruption, and education reform.

\r\n

Q3: How is Gen Z influencing Nepalese society?
A: Through social media campaigns, protests, and creative activism, they are raising awareness, influencing public opinion, and pressuring leaders to rethink policies.

\r\n

Q4: Why is this movement called a "revolution"?
A: It is considered a revolution because it represents a significant shift in how young people engage with society and politics—leveraging technology, challenging traditional norms, and demanding systemic change.

\r\n

Q5: How can ordinary citizens support the Gen Z Revolution?
A: Citizens can support by staying informed, amplifying youth-led campaigns on social media, participating in civic initiatives, and promoting inclusivity and social justice in their communities.

FAQ

\r\n

Q1: What is the difference between global warming and climate change?
Global warming refers to the rise in Earth’s average temperature, while climate change includes global warming and its broader effects like changes in rainfall patterns, stronger storms, and altered ecosystems.

\r\n

Q2: Who is most affected by global warming?
Developing countries, coastal communities, and poorer populations are most vulnerable, as they lack resources to adapt to extreme climate events.

\r\n

Q3: Can individual actions really make a difference?
Yes. If billions of people make small sustainable choices—like reducing plastic use, saving electricity, or using public transport—it creates a massive positive impact globally.

\r\n

Q4: How much time do we have to stop global warming?
Scientists warn that we must take urgent action within this decade (by 2030–2035) to keep global warming under 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels and avoid catastrophic consequences.

\r\n

Q5: What role does technology play in fighting global warming?
Technology helps through renewable energy systems, electric vehicles, carbon capture, smart agriculture, and AI-powered climate modeling that predicts risks and suggests solutions.
